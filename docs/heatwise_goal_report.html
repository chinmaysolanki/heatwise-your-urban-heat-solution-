Mission

Core Mission Statement
To make every rooftop in India a cooling asset — using AI-driven species recommendations, AR-assisted measurement, and a vetted green installer network — so that urban communities can fight extreme heat without waiting for government intervention.

The Problem

Indian cities regularly exceed 45°C in summer. The Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect amplifies ambient temperatures by 3–8°C. Over 90% of Indian rooftops are bare concrete — a massive untapped thermal mass radiating heat into homes and streets. No accessible, AI-guided product exists that takes a homeowner from their specific rooftop conditions to a species plan, installer quote, and verified outcome — until HeatWise.

The Solution

For Homeowners & Businesses
  • Scan rooftop via camera and AR measurement
  • Receive AI-ranked green cover recommendations
  • Visualize before/after thermal transformation
  • Connect with and pay verified green installers
  • Track CO₂ offset, cooling delta, and biodiversity
For the Platform
  • Hybrid ML + rules recommendation engine
  • 73-model data layer for telemetry and outcomes
  • Installer marketplace with SLA and outcome tracking
  • Longitudinal follow-up at 7d / 30d / 90d / 180d
  • Full commercial analytics and unit economics

What We Are Trying to Achieve
Six primary goal pillars drive every product, engineering, and commercial decision at HeatWise. Each goal has a clear scope, success criteria, and dependency chain.
Goal 1 — Deliver a Best-in-Class AI Recommendation Engine
Build a hybrid ML + rules-based recommendation system that produces accurate, reproducible, and explainable green cover plans for any Indian rooftop — regardless of size, budget, climate zone, or soil condition. The engine handles supply constraints, seasonal availability, and budget fit in real time.
ML + Rules HybridTelemetry-FirstExplainable AI
Goal 2 — Build a Verified Installer Marketplace
Create a trust-first installer network covering Tier 1–3 Indian cities. Each installer is capability-verified, SLA-tracked, and commercially integrated. Quotes, job assignments, completion, and outcome tracking all flow through the platform with full auditability.

Every completed installation feeds longitudinal outcome data back into the platform. Temperature delta, plant survival rate, CO₂ offset, and user satisfaction are measured at 7d, 30d, 90d, and 180d checkpoints. This data trains the next generation of the ML model.

Ship a complete 27-screen mobile-first application on Android (Capacitor shell) and web. The app supports phone OTP auth, AR measurement, 3D garden layout visualization, push notifications, and offline-capable project management.

Drive revenue through installer commissions (15% per job), premium PDF reports, and B2B white-label partnerships. Track funnel metrics and unit economics per project to reach contribution-positive economics within 12 months of commercial launch.

All user data is managed under a full consent and retention policy framework. Telemetry is append-only and immutable. Platform audit trails, idempotency records, and structured error handling ensure the system is compliant, debuggable, and production-scalable from day one.

Architecture & Engineering Objectives
The technical goals underpinning the platform's scalability, accuracy, reproducibility, and operational reliability.

Recommendation Engine

ML Layer Targets
  • Python ML service co-deployed with Next.js, scoring species via IPC
  • ML registry versioning with per-rollout guardrail checks
  • Nightly telemetry pipeline export to training data store
  • Candidate snapshot immutability for reproducibility
  • A/B experiment assignment with rollout control flags
  • Automatic fallback on guardrail failure detection
Fallback Layer Targets
  • Catalog hybrid fallback covers 100% of species
  • Rules-only emergency fallback for ML outages
  • Eligibility filtering before candidate scoring
  • Supply constraint injection at recommendation time
  • Budget fit assessment with downgrade path logic
  • Seasonal availability window enforcement

Platform Hardening Targets

Reliability
  • Idempotency — all mutation APIs deduplicated via IdempotencyRecord
  • Structured errors — machine-readable codes + provenance on every error
  • Audit trail — PlatformAuditEvent captures all operations append-only
  • Readiness check — /api/platform/readiness validates DB, ML, and supply before launch
CI / Quality Gates
  • TypeScript strict — tsc --noEmit runs on every push to main
  • Species mapping CI — generated artifact verified in pipeline
  • Recommendation tests — integration suite requires seeded DB
  • ML smoke test — ml:serving-smoke validates Python serving on each deploy

Risk Register & Mitigation Strategies
Key risks across product, technical, commercial, and market dimensions — each with a defined mitigation path built into the platform architecture.
RiskCategorySeverityMitigation
ML model underperforms on edge-case Indian climates (coastal, hill stations) Technical High Catalog hybrid and rules-only fallbacks always active. Guardrail failures trigger automatic downgrade with zero user-facing impact.
Installer network too sparse at launch in Tier-2 cities Commercial High Launch restricted to 5 Tier-1 cities. Tier-2 expansion gated on minimum 3 verified installers per city.
AR measurement inaccurate on low-end Android devices Product Medium Manual measurement fallback always available. AR is an enhancement, not a hard requirement for recommendation generation.
Species availability data stale or mismatched to region Data Medium RecommendationConstraintSnapshot captures supply state at recommendation time. Seasonal windows enforced server-side before candidates are scored.
Plant survival rate below prediction in extreme heat zones Outcome Medium LongitudinalFollowupSchedule tracks survival at 4 checkpoints. Outcome data directly feeds the ML retraining pipeline.
User drop-off before completing recommendation flow Product Medium RecommendationTelemetryEvent tracks drop-off at each stage. Phase 4 Flutter redesign targets conversion friction directly.
Recommendation engine latency unacceptable on mobile Technical Low Python ML subprocess pre-warmed on server start. IdempotencyRecord deduplicates repeat requests. Fingerprinting caches recent responses.
OpenAI API cost spike at high recommendation volume Commercial Low ML-first architecture reduces GPT dependency to edge cases only. Rules fallback costs zero. Cost-per-recommendation tracked in unit economics model.

Species Cooling Benchmark

Target: Top-10 Cooling Species Deployed in Every City
The SpeciesCatalog ranks plants by cooling score (0–10). HeatWise targets deployment of the top 10 catalog species — Vetiver, Areca Palm, Bamboo, Tulsi, Curry Leaf, Bougainvillea, Moringa, Jasmine, Ficus, and Neem — across all active cities. Vetiver carries the highest cooling score at 9/10 with a measured -4°C surface reduction. The recommendation engine weights these species higher in heat-risk zones where UHI score exceeds 7.

City-Level Contribution Model

HeatWise tracks each installation's fractional contribution to the host city's annual green cover target. The City Heat Map screen surfaces this in real time — showing the aggregate cooling effect of all verified installations across a city. This data becomes the platform's most defensible asset: a ground-truth, installation-by-installation record of urban temperature reduction.

Long-Term Vision — 3 Years
By 2028, HeatWise aims to have contributed to measurable UHI reduction in at least 3 major Indian cities — verifiable via satellite thermal imagery comparison before and after platform deployment. The longitudinal outcome data and city-level aggregation make HeatWise uniquely positioned to demonstrate population-scale climate impact backed by ground-truth installation records — a capability no other platform in the market currently offers.

Closing Statement
HeatWise is not a gardening app. It is urban climate infrastructure — built from the ground up for scale, accuracy, and verifiable real-world impact. Every architectural decision, from immutable telemetry to longitudinal outcome tracking, exists to ensure that every claim the platform makes about cooling a city is backed by auditable, ground-truth data. The goal is not to sell green installations. The goal is to make Indian rooftops a measurable weapon against urban heat — and to prove it with numbers.
